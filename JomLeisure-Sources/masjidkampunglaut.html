MASJID KAMPUNG LAUT

The Kampung Laut Mosque is located at the Center for Islamic Studies, University of Malaya (UM) Nilam Puri and is recognized by the National Archives Department as the oldest mosque in Southeast Asia. The Kampung Laut Mosque was also built around the 18th century without the use of nails and structures. This historic place in Kelantan has similarities to the Demak Mosque in East Java; Kono Mosque in Champa as well as Nad Tanjung Mosque and Wadi Hussien Mosque in Thailand. Historically, due to the great flood in the state in January 1967, part of the mosque site in Kampung Laut is said to have fallen into the river. As a result, the original mosque became sloping and shaky. Reconstruction was carried out in November 1968 and completed in November 1969. The architectural height of the Kampung Laut Mosque is a unique design, using no nails but using pieces of wood and bamboo as peg
